1. The dimensional formula of impulse is:
A. [MLT⁻¹]
B. [ML²T⁻²]
C. [MLT⁻²]
D. [M⁰LT⁻¹]
Answer: A  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensions
Explanation: Impulse equals force × time, so its dimensions are [MLT⁻¹].

2. The dimensional formula of surface tension is:
A. [MT⁻²]
B. [MLT⁻²]
C. [ML⁻¹T⁻²]
D. [ML²T⁻²]
Answer: A  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensions
Explanation: Surface tension is force per unit length.

3. The dimensional formula of coefficient of viscosity is:
A. [ML⁻¹T⁻¹]
B. [MLT⁻¹]
C. [ML⁻¹T⁻²]
D. [M⁰L²T⁻¹]
Answer: A  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensions
Explanation: From viscous force = ηA(dv/dx), η has dimensions [ML⁻¹T⁻¹].

4. The dimensional formula of Planck constant is:
A. [ML²T⁻¹]
B. [ML²T⁻²]
C. [MLT⁻¹]
D. [M⁰L²T⁻¹]
Answer: A  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensions
Explanation: From E=hν, h=E/ν.

5. The dimensional formula of gravitational constant G is:

6. Which pair has the same dimensions?
A. Work and torque
B. Pressure and force
C. Angular momentum and energy
D. Impulse and power
Answer: A  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensions
Explanation: Both work and torque have dimensions [ML²T⁻²], though they are different physical quantities.

7. Which pair does not have the same dimensions?
A. Stress and pressure
B. Momentum and impulse
C. Energy and torque
D. Power and angular momentum
Answer: D  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensions
Explanation: Power is [ML²T⁻³], while angular momentum is [ML²T⁻¹].

8. If force F, velocity v and time t are fundamental quantities, the dimensions of mass are:
A. [Fvt⁻¹]
B. [Ft v⁻¹]
C. [Fv⁻¹t]
D. [Fv t]
Answer: B  |  Level: JEE Main  |  Topic: Dimensional analysis
Explanation: F=ma and a=v/t, so m=Ft/v.

9. If density ρ, speed v and area A are fundamental quantities, the dimension of mass is:
A. [ρA³ᐟ²]
B. [ρA]
C. [ρA²]
D. [ρvA]
Answer: A  |  Level: JEE Main  |  Topic: Dimensional analysis
Explanation: Area has dimension L², so volume is A³ᐟ² and mass=ρA³ᐟ².

10. A quantity X is given by X=at²+bt+c, where X is length. Dimensions of b are:
A. [LT⁻¹]
B. [LT⁻²]
C. [L]
D. [T⁻¹]
Answer: A  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensional homogeneity
Explanation: Each term must have dimensions of length, so bt has dimension L.

11. In y=A sin(ωt+kx), dimensions of k are:

Answer: B  |  Level: NEET  |  Topic: Dimensionless arguments

12. In P=a+bv², where P is pressure and v is speed, dimensions of b are:
