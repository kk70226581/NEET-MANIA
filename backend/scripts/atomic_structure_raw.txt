Electromagnetic radiation
1. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
The correct relation between wavelength, frequency and speed of light is:
A. (c=\nu/\lambda)
B. (c=\nu\lambda)
C. (c=\lambda/\nu)
D. (c=\nu+\lambda)
Answer: B
Explanation: Electromagnetic radiation travels with speed (c=\nu\lambda).
2. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
The frequency of radiation having wavelength (600) nm is:
A. (5\times10^{14}\text{ s}^{-1})
B. (2\times10^{14}\text{ s}^{-1})
C. (5\times10^{12}\text{ s}^{-1})
D. (2\times10^{16}\text{ s}^{-1})
Answer: A
Explanation: (\nu=c/\lambda=3\times10^8/(600\times10^{-9})).
3. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
The wavelength of radiation with frequency (6\times10^{14}\text{ s}^{-1}) is:
A. (200) nm
B. (300) nm
C. (500) nm
D. (600) nm
Answer: C
Explanation: (\lambda=c/\nu=5\times10^{-7}) m (=500) nm.
4. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
Which electromagnetic radiation has the highest frequency?
A. Infrared
B. Visible light
C. Ultraviolet
D. Microwave
Answer: C
Explanation: Frequency increases from microwave to infrared, visible and ultraviolet.
5. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
Which radiation has the longest wavelength?
A. X-rays
B. Ultraviolet rays
C. Microwaves
D. Gamma rays
Answer: C
Explanation: Microwaves have much longer wavelengths than UV, X-rays and gamma rays.
6. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]

7. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]

Photoelectric effect
16. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
Photoelectric emission occurs only when the incident radiation has frequency:
A. Lower than threshold frequency
B. Equal to or greater than threshold frequency
C. Independent of threshold frequency
D. Equal to zero
Answer: B
Explanation: A minimum photon energy is required to remove an electron.
17. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
The minimum frequency required for photoelectric emission is called:
A. Resonance frequency
B. Threshold frequency
C. Orbital frequency
D. Wave number
Answer: B
Explanation: Below the threshold frequency, photoelectrons are not emitted.
18. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
The work function of a metal is equal to:
A. (h/\nu_0)
B. (h\nu_0)
C. (hc\nu_0)
D. (h\lambda_0)
Answer: B
Explanation: (\phi=h\nu_0), where (\nu_0) is the threshold frequency.
19. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
Einstein’s photoelectric equation is:
A. (h\nu=\phi-K_{\max})
B. (h\nu=\phi+K_{\max})
C. (h\nu=K_{\max}-\phi)
D. (h\nu=\phi K_{\max})
Answer: B
Explanation: Photon energy is used to overcome the work function and provide kinetic energy.
20. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
Increasing the intensity of light above threshold frequency primarily increases:
A. Maximum kinetic energy of electrons
B. Number of emitted photoelectrons
C. Work function
D. Threshold frequency
Answer: B
Explanation: Greater intensity means more photons striking the surface per unit time.
21. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
Increasing the frequency of incident light above threshold primarily increases:
A. Number of atoms in the metal
B. Maximum kinetic energy of photoelectrons
C. Work function
D. Electron charge
Answer: B
Explanation: (K_{\max}=h\nu-\phi).
22. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
A metal has threshold frequency (4\times10^{14}\text{ s}^{-1}). It is irradiated with light of frequency (6\times10^{14}\text{ s}^{-1}). The maximum kinetic energy is:
A. (6.626\times10^{-20}) J
B. (1.325\times10^{-19}) J
C. (2.650\times10^{-19}) J
D. (3.975\times10^{-19}) J
Answer: B
Explanation: (K_{\max}=h(\nu-\nu_0)=h(2\times10^{14})).
23. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
If incident light has exactly the threshold frequency, the maximum kinetic energy of emitted electrons is:
A. Zero
B. (h\nu)
C. Equal to work function
D. Infinite
Answer: A
Explanation: All photon energy is used to overcome the work function.
24. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
Light of frequency below the threshold is made extremely intense. Photoelectric emission will:
A. Occur with high kinetic energy
B. Occur after a time delay
C. Not occur
D. Increase the work function
Answer: C
Explanation: Each photon individually lacks sufficient energy.
25. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
The photoelectric effect supports the:
A. Purely wave nature of light
B. Particle nature of light
C. Nuclear model of atom
D. Existence of neutrons
Answer: B
Explanation: Light transfers energy through individual photons.
Hydrogen spectrum and Bohr model
26. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
The line spectrum of hydrogen arises because electrons:
A. Can possess any energy
B. Occupy only discrete energy levels
C. Remain stationary in the nucleus
D. Emit radiation continuously

Explanation: Each spectral line corresponds to a transition between fixed energy levels.

Dual nature and uncertainty principle
51. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Easy]
The de Broglie wavelength of a moving particle is:
A. (p/h)
B. (h/p)
C. (hp)
D. (h+p)
Answer: B
Explanation: Matter wavelength is inversely proportional to momentum.
52. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
If the momentum of a particle is doubled, its de Broglie wavelength becomes:
A. Double
B. Half
C. Four times
D. Unchanged
Answer: B
Explanation: (\lambda=h/p).
53. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
Two particles have equal momentum. Their de Broglie wavelengths are:
A. Equal
B. Inversely proportional to mass
C. Directly proportional to charge
D. Inversely proportional to velocity only
Answer: A
Explanation: Wavelength depends only on momentum.
54. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
An electron and a proton have the same kinetic energy. Which has the greater de Broglie wavelength?
A. Proton
B. Electron
C. Both equal
D. Cannot be predicted
Answer: B
Explanation: (\lambda=h/\sqrt{2mK}), so the lighter electron has greater wavelength.
55. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
An electron and a proton move with the same velocity. The ratio (\lambda_e/\lambda_p) is approximately:
A. (1)
B. (m_e/m_p)
C. (m_p/m_e)
D. (\sqrt{m_p/m_e})
Answer: C
Explanation: At equal velocity, wavelength is inversely proportional to mass.
56. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
The de Broglie wavelength of an electron accelerated through potential (V) is proportional to:
A. (V)
B. (1/V)
C. (\sqrt V)
D. (1/\sqrt V)
Answer: D
Explanation: Its kinetic energy is (eV), so momentum varies as (\sqrt V).
57. [NEET/AIPMT Pattern | Medium]
